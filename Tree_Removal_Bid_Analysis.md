## 2. Key Gaps and Unknowns by Contractor

### First State Tree ($4,000)
*   **The Good:** This is currently the most comprehensive and transparent bid. It explicitly includes the 10 dead ash trees at no additional charge and avoids the logistical risks of a crane.
*   **The Unknown:** Are there any hidden costs for stump grinding (vs. just "flushing" them low to the ground)?

### Tremendous Tree ($9,600 full scope / $3,200 big trees only)
*   **The Good:** Gareth Peoples is an ISA Certified Arborist, bringing high-level expertise. The bid is intentionally structured in two parts, allowing for the option to hire them *only* for the big trees (pines + cherry) at a competitive $3,200.
*   **The Unknown:** The ash tree pricing ($6,400) is exceptionally high and non-competitive. Driveway access will be impeded, implying heavy equipment use, and a crane may be utilized if both items are purchased.

### AC Tree Experts ($2,700 base / $3,300 with logs)
*   **The Good:** Lowest base price for the primary trees (pines and cherry). No crane required.
*   **The Unknown:** Ash trees are entirely excluded and will require a separate future job, which will ultimately increase the total project cost. Availability is pushed out to May.

### Roger Summers ($3,300)
*   **The Good:** Known and trusted contractor.
*   **The Unknown:** The bid is based on time (half-day crane) rather than a guaranteed scope. If the crane work takes longer than expected, costs could double to the full-day rate ($6,500). Ash trees are excluded. Furthermore, crane logistics (operator control, weather contingencies, footprint) remain unaddressed.

### Tree Huggerz ($8,800)
*   **The Good:** Likely covers the entire property.
*   **The Unknown:** The quote lacks a detailed breakdown. It is unclear if this high price is due to a full-day crane rental, the inclusion of the ash trees, or simply higher margins.

## 4. Strategic Overview and Recommendations

At this stage, **First State Tree ($4,000)** remains the clear frontrunner for the *full property scope*. Their bid offers the highest value by including the 10 dead ash trees at no extra cost, effectively solving the entire property's tree issues in a single mobilization. Furthermore, their "no crane" approach eliminates significant logistical risks, third-party dependencies, and potential property damage associated with heavy equipment.

**Tremendous Tree** introduces an interesting dynamic. While their full scope ($9,600) is the highest bid and their ash tree pricing ($6,400) is non-competitive, their Item 1 price for the big trees only ($3,200) is very competitive. If the decision is made to defer the ash trees, Tremendous Tree becomes a strong contender for the primary job, especially given Gareth's ISA Certified Arborist credential.

While AC Tree Experts ($2,700) appears cheaper initially, adding log removal ($600) and a future separate job for the 10 ash trees will almost certainly push their total cost well above First State Tree's $4,000.

Roger Summers presents too much scope risk (paying for time rather than completed work) and Tree Huggerz is currently priced out of contention unless their scope reveals significant hidden value.
